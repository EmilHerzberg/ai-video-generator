# 01 — Brand Identity (Positioning & Voice)

> Personal-brand specifics are tokenized. Substitute before use:
> `{{BRAND_NAME}}` = display name · `{{BRAND_MONOGRAM}}` = monogram · `{{BRAND_SUBTITLE}}` = role tagline · `{{BRAND_SIGNATURE}}` = short signature mark · `{{BRAND_EMAIL}}` = contact email.

## Identity in one line

**Advanced infrastructure, built by a human with taste.**

{{BRAND_NAME}} is a *technical operator building intelligent systems*. The voice and visuals project: technical intelligence · calm authority · modern systems thinking · strategic depth · premium editorial design · warm computational aesthetics.

**Balance: 70% technical / 30% warm editorial.**

## What it should feel like

High-end operating system · premium SaaS dashboard · strategic intelligence platform · AI research interface · editorial technology magazine · warm futuristic strategy interface.

Reader reaction target: *"this person understands complex systems, but also knows how to present them clearly"* and *"I learned something useful and uncommon."*

## What it must NEVER feel like

Cold cyberpunk · gamer RGB · sci-fi neon overload · crypto-bro dashboard · generic AI-robot / humanoid imagery · Canva template · motivational-influencer visuals · corporate strategy-deck clip-art.

## Voice

Headlines read as **strategic insight, not marketing slogan**. Bold, compact, short, high-signal phrasing. Strong hierarchy. Generous readability — not sterile.

- Headline: 8–12 words. A specific, non-obvious take. Not a slogan.
- Key insight: ≤18 words.
- Final takeaway: 12–18 words, one-line resolution.
- Every post teaches something **useful and uncommon** — a specific, non-obvious insight anchored in a fact, number, or mechanism. **Topic is open**: technology, science, data, systems, engineering, economics, strategy — any subject with a real analytical point qualifies (AI/automation is the home turf, not a fence). The brand's through-line is the *treatment* — signal-dense, technical, data-driven, calm authority — not a fixed subject area. Never generic business/marketing advice, motivational fluff, or hype.

## Voice anti-patterns (never do these)

- **No hype.** No engagement bait ("comment X to get Y"), no slogan tone, no generic LinkedIn phrasing, no hype list-posts.
- **No motivational / founder-journey / productivity-guru content.**
- **No fake stats.** Illustrative numbers must be labeled `example model` / `conceptual` / `illustrative system model`. Never fabricate business/revenue claims.
- **No emoji spam.** Max 1–3 per graphic, and only when an emoji clarifies faster than text or replaces a longer label. Never in the main headline unless the topic is intentionally light. **Forbidden:** 🚀 🔥 💯 ✨ 🙌 💸 🤯 👀 🤑 and excessive ✅/❌ — they collapse the brand into hype LinkedIn voice.
- **No crypto / gamer / sci-fi imagery**, no AI-robot or humanoid clichés.
- **No smug "myth vs reality" tone.**
- On **Reddit** specifically: audiences are more skeptical of marketing voice — keep the headline technical; engagement-bait phrasing fails harder there.
